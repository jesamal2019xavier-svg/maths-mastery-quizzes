üìö Understanding Significant Figures

Significant figures are the digits that carry meaning in a number:

  • All non-zero digits are significant (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9)
  • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant (e.g., 103 has 3 sf)
  • Leading zeros are NOT significant (e.g., 0.0056 has 2 sf)
  • Trailing zeros:
    • Trailing zeros in a whole number without a decimal point are ambiguous (e.g., 1200 could be 2, 3, or 4 sf)
    • Trailing zeros after a decimal point ARE significant (e.g., 12.00 has 4 sf)
Important: In GCSE, if a question says "round 1200 to 2 significant figures", you would write it as 1200. The trailing zeros are needed to show the place value.
